1. Who We Are

MailVeil is a Chrome browser extension that summarizes Gmail emails using AI while protecting the privacy of the people mentioned in those emails. The extension and website at mail-veil.com are operated by the MailVeil team.

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect through the MailVeil Chrome extension and website, why we collect it, how we use it, and what rights you have.

2. What Data We Collect

We collect the minimum data necessary to provide the service.

Account information (when you sign in with Google)

  • Your Google account email address
  • Your Google account display name
  • Your Google account profile picture URL

This data is used solely to identify your account and is sourced from Google's standard OAuth sign-in. We request only the userinfo.email and userinfo.profile scopes — we do not request access to your emails or any other Google data.

Usage data

  • Monthly summary count — we store the number of email summaries you have generated in the current calendar month. This is used to enforce the free tier limit (5 summaries/month) and the Pro tier limit (200 summaries/month). This count resets at the start of each calendar month.

Event logs

We record the following events for security monitoring and service reliability:

  • Sign-in events
  • Error events (e.g. failed API calls, quota exceeded)
  • Administrative actions (e.g. account deletion)

These logs do not include your email content. They are automatically deleted after 90 days.

Deleted account identifiers

When an account is deleted, we retain the associated Google account email address solely to prevent repeated misuse of the free trial. This data is not used for any other purpose.

Payment data

Subscription payments are processed by Stripe. MailVeil does not store, process, or have access to your credit card or payment details. All payment data is governed by Stripe's Privacy Policy.

3. What We Do Not Collect

🔒 Your email content is never stored on MailVeil servers. The obfuscated text sent to our server for AI processing is discarded immediately after the summary is returned — it is never logged or retained.

  • Email content — We never store the text of your emails, obfuscated or otherwise, on our servers.
  • Obfuscation mappings — The table that maps placeholders (e.g. [PER_1]) back to real names and details exists only in your browser's memory during an active summarization session. It is never transmitted to our servers.
  • Contacts or address book data — We do not access, read, or store your Gmail contacts.
  • Attachment files — PDF and other attachments are processed locally in your browser. File content is not transmitted to or stored by MailVeil.
  • Browsing history — We do not track which websites you visit outside of Gmail.

4. How Your Email Content Is Processed

When you request a summary, the following sequence occurs:

1

Local reading. The email text is read directly from Gmail in your browser. It does not leave your device at this point.

2

On-device entity detection. A BERT-based Named Entity Recognition (NER) model, bundled inside the extension, analyses the text locally to identify names, email addresses, phone numbers, physical addresses, and other personal details. This AI model runs entirely in your browser — no data is sent to a server for this step.

3

Local obfuscation. Detected personal details are replaced with anonymous placeholders (e.g. Sarah Jones becomes [PER_1], 07700 900123 becomes [PHONE_1]). The mapping between placeholders and real values is held only in your browser's memory.

4

Transmission of obfuscated text. Only the anonymised, placeholder-substituted version of the email is sent to MailVeil's server. Real personal details are never transmitted.

5

AI summarisation. MailVeil's server forwards the obfuscated text to the Google Gemini API to generate a summary. The obfuscated text is not stored — it is passed through and discarded immediately after the summary response is received.

6

Local restoration. The summary is returned to your browser. If you choose to reveal original details, the local mapping is used to substitute placeholders back to real names. This happens entirely in your browser.

You can review the obfuscated text before it is sent by using the "Preview before sending" feature in the extension. If you spot any missed personal detail, you can use the manual masking tool to cover it before proceeding.

5. How We Use Your Data

The data we collect is used exclusively for the following purposes:

  • Account management — to authenticate you and associate your usage with your account.
  • Quota enforcement — to track how many summaries you have used in the current month and enforce free/Pro tier limits.
  • Subscription management — to verify your Pro subscription status via Stripe.
  • Fraud prevention — to detect and prevent repeated misuse of the free trial using deleted account identifiers.
  • Service reliability — to detect errors and maintain service stability using anonymised event logs.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or marketing. We do not sell your data to third parties.

6. Third-Party Services

MailVeil uses the following third-party services to operate:

Google Firebase

Used for user authentication (Firebase Auth) and database storage (Firestore). Your account email, display name, profile picture, monthly usage count, and event logs are stored in Firebase. Firebase is operated by Google LLC. See Firebase Privacy and Security.

Google Gemini API

Used to generate email summaries from obfuscated text. MailVeil accesses Gemini through a paid API tier. Under Google's paid API terms, data submitted to the Gemini API is not used to train Google's AI models. See Gemini API Terms of Service.

Stripe

Used to process Pro subscription payments. MailVeil does not store payment card details. All billing data is handled by Stripe, Inc. under their own privacy policy. See Stripe Privacy Policy.

7. Data Retention

Data Retention period
Email content (obfuscated text sent for summarisation) Not retained — discarded immediately after summary is generated
Monthly summary count Reset at the start of each calendar month
Account profile (email, name, picture) Retained until account is deleted
Event logs Automatically deleted after 90 days
Deleted account email identifiers Retained indefinitely solely to prevent free trial abuse

8. Your Rights

Depending on where you are located, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction — request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Deletion — request deletion of your personal data. Note that deleting your account via the extension will delete your profile immediately and revoke your Google authentication token. The email address used will be retained to prevent trial abuse (see section 7).
  • Portability — request your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Objection — object to processing of your data in certain circumstances.

Deleting your account

You can delete your account at any time from within the MailVeil extension by opening the account menu and selecting Close Account. Upon deletion:

  • Your profile (name, picture, usage data) is deleted immediately from our database.
  • Your Google OAuth token is revoked — MailVeil loses all access to your Google account.
  • Your email address is retained solely to prevent re-registration for a new free trial.

To exercise any other rights, or to request deletion of your retained email identifier, contact us at the address in section 12.

9. Cookies

The MailVeil website does not use cookies or any tracking technologies. No analytics, advertising, or tracking scripts are loaded on this website.

The MailVeil Chrome extension uses browser storage (chrome.storage.local) to cache your authentication state locally on your device. This is not a cookie and is not accessible to websites.

10. Children's Privacy

MailVeil is not directed at children under the age of 13 (or 16 in the EU/UK). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. For significant changes, we will notify users via the extension or by email where required by law.

Continued use of MailVeil after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
